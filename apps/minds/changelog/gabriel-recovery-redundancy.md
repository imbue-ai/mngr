Workspace recovery now reads the host's lifecycle state and its provider's reachability from the same passive discovery data the rest of minds already uses (the workspace list, the providers panel), instead of firing its own separate `mngr list` each time the recovery page loads. Having two independent samplers of the same state could let the recovery page disagree with the sidebar; collapsing to one source removes that inconsistency. As a side benefit the recovery page now responds effectively instantly (it reads in-memory state rather than waiting on a network round-trip), and a dropped connection can no longer strand the diagnostic on a slow probe.

The recovery page now updates itself as the situation becomes clear, without a manual reload. In the brief window right after a connection drops -- before discovery has confirmed what happened -- the page shows a neutral "Checking workspace connection" state with a Retry button rather than offering the destructive "Restart workspace" button on stale information. Once discovery catches up (typically within a few seconds), the page transitions on its own to the right action: "Restart workspace" if the workspace genuinely needs one, the "Can't connect to ..." page if the provider is down, an automatic in-place restart where that applies, or straight back to your workspace if it recovered on its own.
