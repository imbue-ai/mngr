The VPS-resident Latchkey gateway and its VPS->container reverse SSH tunnel are now supervised by `supervisord` instead of being spawned detached (`nohup` + a PID-file guard). `supervisord` is installed from the distro package during remote-gateway provisioning, so its init service auto-starts it on boot; both processes are registered as `autostart`/`autorestart` programs, meaning they are restarted if they crash and brought back up after the VM reboots.

The reverse SSH tunnel now carries keepalive and timeout flags (`ServerAliveInterval`, `ServerAliveCountMax`, `TCPKeepAlive`, `ConnectTimeout`, `BatchMode`) so a connection wedged by a paused-then-resumed VM is detected and torn down, letting `supervisord` re-establish a fresh tunnel.

Note: enabling reboot survival required persisting the gateway's encryption key and listen password to 0600 root-owned files on the VPS (read into the gateway's environment by a launch wrapper), since `supervisord` must be able to restart the gateway autonomously after a reboot when no desktop is connected to re-push them. Previously these secrets were never persisted on the VPS disk.
