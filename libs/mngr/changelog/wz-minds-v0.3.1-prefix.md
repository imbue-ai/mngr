Re-pin `cryptography>=42.0,<47` in `libs/mngr/pyproject.toml` to fix the Apple-M5 lima-VZ SIGILL regression.

Root cause (gdb-pinpointed on the M5 launch-to-msg runner; see forever-claude-template PR #151): lima-VZ on Apple M5 advertises SVE/SVE2 in the guest's `/proc/cpuinfo` but traps the SVE `cntb` instruction OpenSSL runs in its CPU-cap init (`_armv8_sve_get_vl_bytes`). `cryptography` 47.0.0+'s Rust openssl binding runs that init at import, so it SIGILLs (exit 132) in the lima-VZ guest -- crashing anything that imports paramiko (mngr CLI, system-interface), i.e. most of the in-VM agent, into recovery. Open upstream issue: https://github.com/pyca/cryptography/issues/14733 (47.0.0 aarch64, Docker/VM on Apple Silicon incl. M4). 46.x imports cleanly. The cap was added in `f3d498bef`, then reverted in `68f3f6b7a` with no stated reason, which silently regressed `launch_to_msg`. Re-confirmed by a controlled experiment on the runner: same binary, FCT `vendor/mngr` pinned `<47` -> full green; unpinned (`48.0.0`) -> W1 recovery/SIGILL. (The alternative root-cause fix, `OPENSSL_armcap=0` to force NEON-only paths and allow newer cryptography, lives on a separate FCT branch and is not wired through here; the pin is the approach the pilot uses.)
