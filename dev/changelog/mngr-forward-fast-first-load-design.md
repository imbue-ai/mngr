Added a design document at `docs/design/forward-fast-first-load.md` investigating why the minds desktop app's window restore for a remote (`imbue_cloud`) mind sits on the `mngr_forward` "Loading workspace..." 503 loader for ~50-55s even when the backing host is healthy, and proposing how to make first-load routing succeed in ~3s.

The doc traces the root cause (first `ForwardResolver.resolve()` success depends on a slow per-agent `mngr event ... services --follow` stream -- a cold `mngr` startup + `imbue_cloud` init + per-agent SSH round trip -- while membership and SSH info are already known ~2.5s in), verifies with file:line evidence where service registrations actually live and when they become knowable (in-container self-registration into `<host_dir>/agents/<id>/events/services/events.jsonl`, co-located with files the discovery poll already reads over its single per-host SSH visit), and compares four directions. It recommends carrying the per-agent service map on the discovery snapshot and seeding the resolver at replay (no new per-agent SSH cost, bounded self-correcting staleness), keeps `mngr_forward` app-agnostic, and argues against persisting a service map across launches (a stale route is worse than no route because it drives minds' STUCK/auto-restart recovery). Design only; no implementation.
